# Professional network research notes

Interviews with Abuja professionals, June 2026. Source for pricing, process
accuracy in the guides, and contractor-network onboarding.

## Lilian Ezeh — Lawyer (Prime Stratus Law)

**Process:**
- Verify document validity first: C of O / R of O, issued by the FCDA. For
  local-government land, the C of O / R of O for that local government is
  still required.
- AGIS is where lawyers verify title documents — C of O, R of O, everything
  about the property is checked there. Lawyers write a letter to AGIS; the
  seller accompanies the lawyer.
- Documents needed: C of O / R of O (C of O most important — plot number and
  everything about the land flows from it), survey plan, TDP (shows the
  landscape of the land).
- Court search to verify whether the property is in dispute.
- Deed of assignment drafted between buyer and owner after verification.
- Lawyers are involved beginning to end; buyers often skip them on cost.

**Accountability angle:** a lawyer can be held accountable — reportable to the
LPDC, can be disbarred. (Useful trust point for the network: our
professionals carry professional liability.)

**Pricing:** 10% of property value up to ₦100m; 5% above ₦100m; negotiable.

**Other:** considers estate plots the safer/easier purchase now. Interested
in joining the network. Asked whether Propersafe is CAC-registered.

## Princess Denedo — Lawyer

- **Estate purchases:** land acquired from government, license issued. Verify
  via estate managers → who owns the land → legitimacy → change-of-ownership
  documents.
- **Non-estate land:** bodies in charge for Abuja are AMAC, FCDA, AGIS.
- **Window search vs formal letters:** registries initially offer informal
  "window searches"; genuine formal letters are obtainable but need a
  contact and cost more than the administrative fee.
- **Pricing:** ~10% of property value, negotiated down at higher prices.
- **Per purchase type:**
  - Ready-built: transfer-of-ownership documents, property managers.
  - Commercial/build: surveyors, developers (some fund builds for interest),
    property manager accountable under legal agreement.
- **Key insight:** need multiple validators on costs to prevent foul play or
  overpricing by the very people you work with; need a trustworthy way to
  facilitate communication. (This is Propersafe's redundancy + coordination
  model, stated unprompted.)

## Abdulazeez Zubairu — Land Surveyor

- Works with real estate companies in Abuja (incl. Promise Land Estate);
  launched "Wet App" (save toward land over time).
- **Verification before purchase:** location, land size, residential vs
  commercial designation.
- **FCDA timelines:** a response can take months; paid fast-track exists as a
  separate fee. Fee depends on land size (larger land = larger fee). Will
  send fee schedule. Has a contact in the FCDA.
- **Lawyer vs surveyor division:** lawyer verifies paper authenticity;
  surveyor verifies the land on the ground (size, designation, location).
- **Abuja masterplan:** surveyors hold the FCT masterplan — scammers sell
  land on pending roads/corridors; masterplan checks catch this.
- Survey types practiced: cadastral, engineering (roads/bridges/drainage),
  hydrographic (water/flooding).
- Has a team incl. a registered construction company; QS prepares bill of
  quantities (end-to-end build costing) and can verify a BOQ.
- **Pricing:** ₦200k / ₦500k / ₦1m by land size; negotiable.
- Two land types in Abuja: FCDA-approved and local land. Location determines
  price; local land = high demand, less developed.
